₿

The Blockchain Evidence Problem

Prosecutors present blockchain evidence as though it is infallible — a perfect, immutable record that proves exactly what they say it proves. It is not. Blockchain analytics involves assumptions, probabilistic inferences, and interpretive choices that can be challenged. Wallet attribution, transaction clustering, and exchange linkage all involve methodology that can be wrong.

Joe knows where those vulnerabilities are. He helped develop the evidentiary standards now being applied in Tennessee courts. That means he also knows exactly where they fall short — and how to use that in your defense.
